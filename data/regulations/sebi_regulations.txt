SECTION 1: INCOME TAX DEDUCTIONS FOR INVESTORS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Section 80C – Maximum Deduction: ₹1,50,000 per year
Eligible Investments:
- ELSS (Equity Linked Savings Scheme): 3-year lock-in, highest return potential
- PPF (Public Provident Fund): 15-year lock-in, 7.1% interest, EEE tax status
- EPF (Employee Provident Fund): Mandatory for salaried employees
- NSC (National Savings Certificate): 5-year lock-in
- Tax-saving FD: 5-year lock-in, taxable interest
- Life Insurance Premium (LIC, term plans)
- Home Loan Principal Repayment
- Sukanya Samridhi Yojana (SSY): For girl child, 8.2% interest
- Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS): 8.2% for 60+ age

Section 80CCD(1B) – Additional NPS deduction: ₹50,000 over and above 80C limit
- National Pension System (NPS) contributions under Tier-I account
- Total effective 80C + 80CCD limit = ₹2,00,000

Section 80D – Health Insurance Premium:
- Self/Family: ₹25,000 per year
- Parents below 60: ₹25,000 additional
- Parents 60+: ₹50,000 additional
- Maximum total: ₹1,00,000

Section 80TTA – Savings Account Interest: ₹10,000 deduction
Section 80TTB – Senior Citizens FD Interest: ₹50,000 deduction

Standard Deduction (Salaried): ₹50,000 (both old and new regime)

SECTION 2: INCOME TAX SLABS FY 2024-25
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

OLD TAX REGIME:
- Up to ₹2,50,000: NIL
- ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000: 5%
- ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000: 20%
- Above ₹10,00,000: 30%
- Rebate u/s 87A: No tax if income ≤ ₹5,00,000

NEW TAX REGIME (Default from FY 2024-25):
- Up to ₹3,00,000: NIL
- ₹3,00,001 – ₹6,00,000: 5%
- ₹6,00,001 – ₹9,00,000: 10%
- ₹9,00,001 – ₹12,00,000: 15%
- ₹12,00,001 – ₹15,00,000: 20%
- Above ₹15,00,000: 30%
- Rebate u/s 87A: No tax if income ≤ ₹7,00,000

Health & Education Cess: 4% on total income tax
Surcharge: 10% on income >₹50L, 15% on >₹1Cr, 25% on >₹2Cr

SECTION 3: CAPITAL GAINS TAX (as of July 2024 Budget)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

EQUITY AND EQUITY MUTUAL FUNDS:
- Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG) – held < 12 months: 20% (increased from 15% in Budget 2024)
- Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG) – held ≥ 12 months: 12.5% without indexation
- LTCG Exemption: First ₹1,25,000 per year is exempt (increased from ₹1L in Budget 2024)

DEBT MUTUAL FUNDS (post April 2023):
- All gains taxed as per income tax slab (removed LTCG benefit)

REAL ESTATE:
- STCG (< 2 years): As per tax slab
- LTCG (≥ 2 years): 20% with indexation OR 12.5% without indexation (as per Budget 2024)

GOLD / GOLD ETF / SOVEREIGN GOLD BONDS:
- SGB interest: Taxable as income; LTCG on redemption: EXEMPT after 8 years
- Gold ETF LTCG (≥ 12 months): 12.5% without indexation

STT (Securities Transaction Tax):
- Equity Delivery: 0.1% on buy and sell
- Futures: 0.0125% on sell side
- Options: 0.0625% on sell side (premium)

SECTION 4: SEBI MUTUAL FUND REGULATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996 and subsequent amendments:

Fund Categories (SEBI Categorization Circular 2017):
EQUITY FUNDS:
- Large Cap: Must invest ≥80% in top 100 companies by market cap
- Mid Cap: Must invest ≥65% in 101-250 ranked companies
- Small Cap: Must invest ≥65% in 251+ ranked companies
- Multi Cap: Min 25% each in large/mid/small cap
- Flexi Cap: Flexible allocation across market caps
- ELSS: Min 80% in equity, 3-year lock-in

DEBT FUNDS:
- Liquid Fund: Maturity ≤91 days; no entry/exit load after 7 days
- Overnight Fund: Maturity = 1 day
- Short Duration: 1-3 year Macaulay duration
- Corporate Bond Fund: Min 80% in AA+ and above

HYBRID FUNDS:
- Balanced Advantage Fund (BAF): Dynamic equity allocation
- Aggressive Hybrid: 65-80% equity

KYC Requirements:
- PAN mandatory for investments above ₹50,000
- Aadhaar-based e-KYC allowed
- FATCA declaration required

SIP Rules:
- Minimum SIP: ₹500/month (most AMCs)
- SEBI mandated SIP pause facility (3 months)
- 30-day cooling-off period for SIP cancellation

Expense Ratios (TER) – Maximum Limits:
- Equity: 2.25% for AUM ≤₹500Cr, reducing to 1.05% for >₹50,000Cr
- Debt: 2.00% (lower than equity)
- Direct plans: 0.75-1.0% lower than regular plans

EXIT LOAD:
- Most equity funds: 1% if redeemed within 1 year
- ELSS: No exit load (but 3-year lock-in)
- Liquid/Overnight: No exit load after 7 days

SECTION 5: NPS (NATIONAL PENSION SYSTEM)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Regulated by: PFRDA (Pension Fund Regulatory and Development Authority)

Account Types:
- Tier I: Pension account, mandatory minimum ₹1,000/year
- Tier II: Voluntary savings, no lock-in (no tax benefit)

Tax Benefits:
- Contribution up to 10% of salary (Basic+DA) under 80CCD(1): Within 80C limit
- Additional ₹50,000 under 80CCD(1B): Over and above 80C
- Employer contribution up to 10% of salary: Tax-free income

Investment Options:
- Auto Choice: Age-based allocation (default)
- Active Choice: Manual allocation
  - Equity (E): Max 75% (for age <50), reduces by 2.5%/year after 50
  - Corporate Bonds (C): Rated AA and above
  - Government Securities (G): Central/State Govt bonds
  - Alternative Assets (A): Max 5%

Pension Fund Managers: LIC, SBI, HDFC, ICICI, UTI, Kotak, Aditya Birla, Axis

Withdrawal Rules:
- At 60: Min 40% must be used for annuity; 60% lump sum is TAX-FREE
- Partial withdrawal: Allowed after 3 years for specific reasons (education, marriage, medical)
- Tax treatment: 60% lump sum is exempt; annuity income taxable

Historical Returns (5-year average):
- Equity (E): 14-16% CAGR
- Corporate Bonds (C): 8-10%
- Government Securities (G): 7-9%

SECTION 6: PPF (PUBLIC PROVIDENT FUND)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Current Interest Rate: 7.1% per annum (Q1 FY 2024-25, announced quarterly by government)
Lock-in: 15 years (extendable in 5-year blocks)
Investment Limit: Minimum ₹500, Maximum ₹1,50,000 per year
EEE Status: Exempt-Exempt-Exempt (investment, interest, maturity all tax-free)

Partial Withdrawal: Allowed from 7th year (up to 50% of balance)
Loan Facility: 3rd to 6th year (interest = PPF rate + 1%)

Best For: Conservative investors, those in 30% tax bracket (effective yield = 7.1/0.7 = ~10.1%)

SECTION 7: SEBI INVESTMENT ADVISER REGULATIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

SEBI (Investment Advisers) Regulations, 2013:
- Any entity providing investment advice must be registered with SEBI
- IA Registration: Mandatory for fee-based advice
- Qualifications: NISM Series-X-A/B certification required
- Fee Disclosure: Fixed fee or AUM-based fee (not commission-based)
- Conflict of Interest: Cannot distribute products they advise on

AMFI (Association of Mutual Funds in India):
- All mutual fund distributors must be AMFI-registered (ARN number)
- NISM Series V-A exam mandatory for distributors

RBI Regulations on Banking:
- Savings Account Interest: RBI deregulated; typically 3-4% (major banks) to 7% (small finance banks)
- FD Rates: FDIC not applicable in India; DICGC insures up to ₹5,00,000 per bank per depositor

SECTION 8: RECOMMENDED ASSET ALLOCATION BY RISK PROFILE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CONSERVATIVE (Low Risk):
- Debt/Fixed Income: 70% (FDs, Debt MFs, Bonds)
- Equity: 20% (Large Cap / Index Funds)
- Gold: 10% (SGBs, Gold ETF)

MODERATE (Balanced):
- Equity: 60% (50% large cap + 10% mid cap)
- Debt: 30% (Short-duration MFs, FDs)
- Gold: 10%

AGGRESSIVE (High Risk, Long Horizon):
- Equity: 80% (30% large + 30% mid + 20% small cap)
- Debt: 15% (For liquidity)
- Gold: 5%

Emergency Fund: 6 months of expenses in liquid fund or savings account (always maintain first)
Insurance: Term life cover = 10-15x annual income; Health cover ≥ ₹5,00,000
